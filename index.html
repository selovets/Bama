Read My Biography

Early Life & Education

My name is Bamlaku Behailu Yeshite, born on December 3, 1996, in Addis Ababa, Ethiopia. I began my educational journey at Repi Primary and Secondary School in Kara Kore, where I built a strong academic foundation that would shape my future.

Growing up, I learned the value of hard work early. During summer breaks, I worked in the construction sector to cover my school expenses—saving up to buy uniforms and stationery. These formative experiences taught me resilience, discipline, and the importance of self-reliance.

Academic Excellence

After completing high school, I joined Wollega University to pursue a degree in Public Health. My dedication to academics paid off when I graduated with the highest score in my department, achieving an impressive CGPA of 3.91 out of 4.0.

My exceptional performance earned me an offer to become a lecturer at the university—a position I turned down at the time as my dream was to gain practical experience and eventually become an Integrated Emergency Surgical Officer (IESO).

"I serve the community well. I give service from my heart. I am very responsible and have empathy for patients. Many patients have loved me for my service, and I'm admired and loved by people. There's no organization that fired me except the Federal Ministry of Health contract termination."

Professional Resilience

After graduating, I faced a seven-month period of unemployment—a challenging time that tested my resilience. During this period, I took on various small jobs to stay active, including working on the Sidama People's Referendum where I served on a short-term contract.

My entrepreneurial spirit led me to launch ETRSS1 Home-to-Home Tutorial with a friend. We hired female teachers and offered private tutoring services, building momentum until COVID-19 disrupted our plans and forced us to close.

Continuous Growth

When the pandemic hit, I was employed by the Federal Ministry of Health to support the national COVID-19 response. For 2 years and 7 months, I worked across various sites and organizations, contributing significantly to Ethiopia's fight against the pandemic.

My journey continued with roles in private clinics, insurance underwriting, and participation in the Wolaita People's Referendum. Alongside my professional roles, I earned certifications in Digital Marketing, E-commerce, and AI—skills that opened new professional doors.

Today, I serve as an IMNCI Focal Point at a health center, where I'm appreciated by mothers and families for my compassionate care and quality service. My ability to communicate clearly and build trust within the community has been key to my success.
